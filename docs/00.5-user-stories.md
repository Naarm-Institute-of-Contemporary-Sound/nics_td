# User Stories

This project was originally developed around a personal obsession: creating synchronised audiovisual performances that integrate light, sound and visuals, with a focus on tightly timed lighting and always different generative visuals that run on the fly.

While setting up the NICS I looked for a way to enable the naarm creative community to engage with the studios equipment in the most frictionless way that still enabled open ended output, documenting, simplifying and publishing my workflow seemed like the best way to achieve this.

If any of these sound like you, I hope you can engage with these tools in a rewarding manner:

---

# I am a musician and I want visuals for my music

I have music but little or no experience creating visuals.

I want to:

- Generate visuals that respond to my music
- Understand what parts of my music are driving the visuals
- Experiment with different visual styles
- Create performances without hiring a dedicated visual artist

This course helps by introducing audio analysis, MIDI assignment to lighting and visual mapping workflows that can be applied to any piece of music.

---

# I am a curious beginner and I want to understand how modern AV systems work

I have seen audiovisual performances but do not know how they are built.

I want to:

- Learn the terminology
- Understand the workflow
- Build my first project
- Gain confidence experimenting independently

This course helps by introducing concepts progressively, starting with simple examples and gradually building towards complete shows.

---

# I am a creative technologist and I want reusable building blocks

I enjoy combining software, hardware and artistic ideas.

I want to:

- Reuse components in future projects
- Understand how systems fit together
- Build my own workflows
- Extend and modify existing tools

This course helps by exposing the entire pipeline rather than treating the system as a black box.

---

# I want to perform at NICS (my fav <3)

I want to create work that can eventually be presented to a live audience.

I want to:

- Develop ideas at home
- Preview them before performing
- Test them safely
- Share them with others

This course helps by providing a pathway from experimentation to performance.

Participants can create visualisations, develop complete shows, and ultimately present their work to the NICS community.
